Typography is the voice of your design. It shapes how users perceive and interact with your content. Good typography is invisible—readers should focus on the message, not the mechanism.

## The Principles

### Hierarchy

Every piece of text needs a role. Headings indicate structure; body text conveys meaning; captions add context. Establish clear visual hierarchy so readers can scan and find what they need.

### Readability vs Legibility

**Legibility** is whether you can tell one letter from another. **Readability** is how easy it is to read long passages. Both matter:

- Line length: 45-75 characters
- Line height: 1.4-1.6 for body text
- Font size: 16px minimum for body

### Contrast

High contrast aids readability, but too much strains the eyes. The sweet spot is high enough to be legible, low enough to be comfortable.

```
# The ideal contrast ratio is 4.5:1 for normal text
# 3:1 for large text
```

## Practical Tips

1. **Use a scale** - Set font sizes in a mathematical progression
2. **Limit typefaces** - Two fonts maximum
3. **Test on devices** - What works on desktop may fail on mobile

The best typography makes you forget it's there.
